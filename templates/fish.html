Tips to Take Care

  • Monitor changes for illnesses (like swimming weird)
  • Moniter water quality, make sure there are no fish bullies, and don't make loud noices or sudden temperature changes
  • Make sure you have a filter depending on the size of the tank, and a tank depending on the fish type
  • You can use mechanical filtration, biological filtration, or chemical filtration
  • Substrates, the loose material on the bottom of the tank, is needed. Sand is the better choice since there is no room for contaminants to build up within crevaces
  • Include plants for the fish to play around. Plastic and natural can be used but natural is better due to health benefits
  • Add decorates for the fish to hang out in but be careful with overcrowding
  • Avoid taking fish out as much as possible but the tank should be cleaned every 1 or 2 weeks
  • Fish flakes are best for smaller fish and pellets are best for bottom feeders
  • Feed them lots of protein and some fat too
